---
title: "Conditional Vaults"
description: "MetaDAO v0.4 conditional vaults — splitting KASS and USDC into pass/fail conditional tokens, and redeeming them once the question resolves."
---

A challenge market prices the proposer's stake **conditional** on two mutually
exclusive futures: the claim survives (**pass**) or the claim is disqualified
(**fail**). MetaDAO's **conditional vault** program is what makes those conditional
tokens. It takes an underlying token (KASS or USDC), locks it, and mints a matched set
of conditional tokens — one per outcome — that redeem back to the underlying only
after a binary **question** resolves.

All the wire code Kassandra uses to drive this program by hand lives in
`programs/oracles/src/cpi/metadao.rs`.

## Program id

The challenge stack uses the **v0.4.0** conditional vault:

```text
conditional_vault (v0.4.0): VLTX1ishMBbcX3rdBWGssxawAo1Q2X2qxYFYqiGodVg
```

Defined as `CONDITIONAL_VAULT_ID` in `programs/oracles/src/cpi/metadao.rs:56-59`,
and mirrored in the SDK as `EXTERNAL_PROGRAM_IDS.conditionalVault`
(`sdk/src/constants.ts`).

## The two vaults, four conditional tokens

A single challenge market creates **two** conditional vaults sharing **one** binary
question:

| Vault | Underlying | Outcome 0 (pass) | Outcome 1 (fail) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| KASS vault | `oracle.kass_mint` | **pass-KASS** | **fail-KASS** |
| USDC vault | `oracle.usdc_mint` | **pass-USDC** | **fail-USDC** |

Outcome index **0 is always pass, 1 is always fail** (fixed by the composition
order). Each conditional-token mint is a PDA derived under the vault program at
`[b"conditional_token", vault, index]` (idx `0` = pass, `1` = fail).

<Info>
  Splitting is 1:1 across all outcomes. Splitting `N` underlying gives you `N` of the
  pass token **and** `N` of the fail token. You only get the underlying back by
  redeeming after resolution — and only the winning outcome's token pays out.
</Info>

## The key CPI instructions

Discriminators are `sha256("global:<snake_case_name>")[..8]`, listed in
`programs/oracles/src/cpi/metadao.rs:66-94`.

| Instruction | Discriminator (hex) | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `initialize_question` | `f5 97 6a bc 58 2c 41 d4` | Create the binary question; sets the resolver and outcome count. |
| `initialize_conditional_vault` | `25 58 fa d4 36 da e3 af` | Create a vault over one underlying mint; mints the conditional-token mints. |
| `split_tokens` | `4f c3 74 00 8c b0 49 b3` | Lock `amount` underlying, mint `amount` of every outcome's conditional token. |
| `merge_tokens` | `e2 59 fb 79 e1 82 b4 0e` | The inverse of split (burn a full set, reclaim underlying) — pre-resolution. |
| `redeem_tokens` | `f6 62 86 29 98 21 78 45` | After resolution, burn conditional tokens, pay out underlying by the payout ratio. |
| `resolve_question` | `34 20 e0 b3 b4 08 00 f6` | Set the payout numerators — which outcome won. |

### initialize_question

Creates the binary question with `num_outcomes = 2` and a **resolver**. Kassandra
requires the resolver to be the oracle PDA itself, so only the Kassandra program (via
`settle_challenge`) can ever resolve it. The question PDA is
`[b"question", question_id, oracle, [num_outcomes]]` under the vault program.

### initialize_conditional_vault

Creates a vault bound to one `question` and one `underlying_mint`, at PDA
`[b"conditional_vault", question, underlying_mint]`, and initializes the per-outcome
conditional-token mints. Called twice per market — once for KASS, once for USDC.

### split_tokens

`split_tokens { amount: u64 }` moves `amount` underlying into the vault and mints
`amount` of **each** outcome's conditional token to the caller's holder accounts. It
is used in two distinct ways in a challenge:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Client-side: seeding the pools (the challenger's capital)">
    During [composition](/challenge/composing), the challenger splits their **own**
    KASS into pass-KASS + fail-KASS and their **own** USDC into pass-USDC + fail-USDC,
    then uses those to `add_liquidity` to the two AMMs. This is the challenger's capital
    (and impermanent-loss exposure), never the bond's.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="On-chain: splitting the bond (program-signed)">
    `open_challenge` performs a **program-signed** `split_tokens` of `proposer.bond`
    KASS out of `oracle.stake_vault` (authority = the oracle PDA, seeds
    `[b"oracle", nonce_le, [bump]]`) into the KASS vault, minting pass-KASS + fail-KASS
    to two **oracle-PDA-owned** holders, `market.oracle_pass_kass` and
    `oracle_fail_kass` (`programs/oracles/src/processor/open_challenge.rs:283-335`).
    The bond's conditional tokens then sit **idle** — never traded, never LP'd — so the
    bond carries no impermanent loss. `total_oracle_stake` is intentionally **not**
    decremented (`open_challenge.rs:286-288`).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### redeem_tokens

`redeem_tokens` takes **no args**. It is gated by `question.is_resolved()`, burns the
holder's **full** balance of **every** outcome's conditional token, and transfers
`Σ_i balance_i × payout_numerators[i] / payout_denominator` of the underlying out. For
a binary pass-win `[1, 0]`: pass redeems 1:1 and fail pays 0; for a fail-win `[0, 1]`
it is symmetric.

Because the bond was split into **both** legs at open and never traded, the redeem is
clean: the **full bond KASS** lands back in `oracle.stake_vault` regardless of the
outcome (`settle_challenge.rs:434-531`). What differs by outcome is where the bond then
goes — see [Settlement](/challenge/settle).

<Note>
  `redeem_tokens` uses the **same** account struct as `split_tokens`
  (`InteractWithVault`), verified against the deployed v0.4 `conditional_vault` source.
  The winning side's holder redeems 1:1; the losing side's holder redeems to 0.
</Note>

### resolve_question

Called by `settle_challenge` as a program-signed CPI, with the payout numerators the
verdict produced: `[1, 0]` to survive (pass wins) or `[0, 1]` to disqualify (fail
wins). Its Borsh arg is a `Vec<u32>` — encoded as `len:u32 LE (== 2)` followed by the
two numerators (`metadao.rs:78-90`).

## Which of these Kassandra CPIs on-chain

Kassandra only invokes **two** of these vault instructions as on-chain CPIs, both
program-signed by the oracle PDA:

- `open_challenge` → `split_tokens` (the bond split; `open_challenge.rs:330`).
- `settle_challenge` → `resolve_question` then `redeem_tokens`
  (`settle_challenge.rs:427, 471`).

`initialize_question` and `initialize_conditional_vault` are invoked **client-side** by
the SDK/app during composition, never as an on-chain CPI.

<Tip>
  Every vault instruction is `#[event_cpi]`: each appends two trailing accounts — the
  `event_authority` PDA (`[b"__event_authority"]`, derived under the vault program) and
  the vault program id itself (`metadao.rs:29-36`).
</Tip>

## SDK

The TypeScript builders for these instructions live under `sdk/src/futarchy/` and are
exposed on the `futarchy.*` namespace:

```ts title="sdk/src/futarchy/instructions.ts"
futarchy.initializeQuestion({ questionId, oracle, numOutcomes, payer });
futarchy.initializeConditionalVault({ question, underlyingMint, payer });
futarchy.splitTokens({ question, vault, vaultUnderlying, authority,
                       userUnderlying, conditionalMints, userConditionalAccounts, amount });
futarchy.mergeTokens(/* ... & { amount } */);
futarchy.redeemTokens(/* InteractWithVaultArgs */);
futarchy.resolveQuestion({ question, oracle, payoutNumerators: [1, 0] });
```

PDA derivers are on `futarchy.pda.*`: `question(questionId, oracle, numOutcomes)`,
`conditionalVault(question, underlyingMint)`, `conditionalTokenMint(vault, index)`, and
`vaultEventAuthority()`. See the [SDK futarchy reference](/sdk/futarchy).
