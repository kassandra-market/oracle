Kassandra is a decentralized, **AI-assisted optimistic oracle** on Solana. It answers
**binary and categorical** questions. The common case is cheap: an uncontested proposal
settles with no AI and no markets. The dispute machinery — fact agreement, AI claims, and
decision markets — only fires when proposers disagree.

The novel idea: **interpretation is fixed at oracle creation**, so disputes reduce to
*which evidence is real and relevant* (objective) rather than *what the evidence means*
(subjective). An AI applies that fixed interpretation to an agreed fact set, and a
MetaDAO-style decision market is the ultimate arbiter that can override a faulty AI claim.

No zkTLS, no TEEs. Honesty is enforced **economically** (KASS staking and slashing) and by
**markets** (the final arbiter of truth).

## How an oracle resolves

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create">
    A creator posts a prompt, immutable interpretation rules, categorical options, and a
    deadline, and pays a dynamic KASS creation fee (burned). Config is immutable after
    creation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Propose">
    After the deadline, proposers submit a categorical value plus a KASS bond — no proofs.
    If everyone agrees, the oracle **resolves** immediately: no AI, no markets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Dispute (only on conflict)">
    Two or more distinct values lock the proposers in and open the dispute: a **fact
    proposal** window, then a disjoint **fact voting** window that freezes the agreed
    evidence set.
  </Step>
  <Step title="AI claims">
    Each locked-in proposer reruns the open-source runner over the agreed facts and
    resubmits a value plus AI-claim metadata (model, params, hashes).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Challenge">
    Every AI claim is challengeable in parallel. A challenge opens a MetaDAO decision
    market; a fail-vs-pass TWAP decides whether the claim is disqualified.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolve or dead-end">
    After the last market settles, the final plurality over surviving proposers is
    computed. If nothing survives (or a tie), the oracle reaches an **Invalid dead-end**,
    resolvable only by KASS governance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The monorepo

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="programs/kassandra" icon="microchip" href="/architecture/program">
    The core Solana program, written in **Pinocchio** (not Anchor). Owns oracle state,
    phases, facts, AI claims, plurality, staking, emissions, and the dynamic fee.
  </Card>
  <Card title="sdk" icon="js" href="/sdk/overview">
    A hand-written TypeScript client — instruction builders, account decoders, and PDA
    helpers. No IDL; layouts are shared with the program.
  </Card>
  <Card title="runner" icon="robot" href="/architecture/runner">
    The open-source AI runner. Applies the fixed interpretation to the agreed facts and
    produces a categorical answer plus verifiable metadata.
  </Card>
  <Card title="app" icon="browser" href="/architecture/dapp">
    The single frontend — both the oracle (`/oracles`) and the prediction market
    (`/markets`) sections, served by one Vite + React dApp.
  </Card>
  <Card title="programs/kassandra-market" icon="chart-line" href="/market/overview">
    A KASS-denominated **AMM prediction market** that wraps MetaDAO v0.4
    vault + amm and defers resolution to the Kassandra oracle. One indexer serves both.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  This repo hosts **two** products: the optimistic oracle documented here, and the
  [**Kassandra Market**](/market/overview) prediction market resolved by it. A single app
  and a single indexer serve both.
</Note>
