title: "Browsing oracles"
description: "The Landing and Oracles pages — browse the oracle dashboard and read on-chain oracle state and phase."

The dApp's read surface works fully disconnected — no wallet needed. Two routes cover browsing: the marketing **Landing** page and the **Oracles** dashboard, which lists every oracle decoded from the chain.

## Landing (`/`)

The route `/` renders `app/src/pages/Landing.tsx` — a read-only marketing page (Hero constellation, HowItWorks, and related sections). Use it as the entry point; the dApp itself lives under `/oracles`.

## Oracles dashboard (`/oracles`)

The route `/oracles` renders `app/src/pages/Oracles.tsx`, the oracle browser and dashboard. It reads the chain through the connection wired to the selected cluster (see [Clusters & RPC](/ops/clusters-and-rpc)): the data layer `app/src/data/oracles.ts` enumerates and decodes oracle accounts via `getProgramAccounts`, wrapped by the `app/src/hooks/useOracles.ts` hook (loading / error / refetch, and a re-fetch when the cluster or connection changes).

### Dashboard stats

Above the grid, a stats strip (`DashboardStats`) is computed by the pure `app/src/lib/oracleStats.ts` `deriveStats` — client-side over the already-fetched list, with **no extra RPC**. It shows the by-phase-group counts, the total bonds/stake at risk across active oracles (raw base units, unscaled), and the resolved count.

### Grid, search, filter, sort

Below the stats is a responsive grid of cards, one per decoded on-chain oracle. Each card shows the phase chip, a relative deadline, proposer/fact/option counts, and (when resolved) the resolved option.

A toolbar composes three client-side operations over the fetched list — search, then filter, then sort:

- **Text search** across oracle fields.
- **Phase-filter chips** — All / Proposal / In dispute / AI claim / Challenged / Resolved (accessible `aria-pressed` toggle buttons).
- **Sort** — by deadline or by bonds at risk.

Everything applies client-side, so filtering and sorting never trigger new RPC calls.

## Oracle detail (`/oracles/:pubkey`)

Opening a card navigates to `/oracles/:pubkey` (`app/src/pages/OracleDetail.tsx`), an editorial layout of one oracle plus its facts, proposers, AI claims, and challenge market, with copy-on-click truncated pubkeys/hashes. The read layout carries:

- **A verdict banner** — Resolved · Option N, dead-ended, or the in-flight phase and what happens next.
- **A phase timeline** — `app/src/components/oracles/PhaseTimeline.tsx` over the pure `app/src/lib/phaseTimeline.ts` model, highlighting the current phase and its deadline, with a distinct dead-end terminal.
- **An economic panel** — `app/src/components/oracles/EconomicPanel.tsx`, flat bars of bond pool vs dispute bonds vs total stake, plus the proposer bond-by-option split.
- **A live challenge-market visualization** — `app/src/components/oracles/ChallengeMarketPanel.tsx` (covered in [Challenge the AI claim](/app/challenge)).

The wallet-signed write forms on this page are additive on top of the read layout; each is covered in [Participate](/app/participate), [Finalize & crank](/app/finalize-and-crank), and [Claim & close](/app/claim-and-close).

### Reading oracle state and phase

The oracle account decodes via the SDK's `decodeOracle` (`sdk/src/accounts/`). Its `phase` field maps to the `Phase` enum — `Created`, `Proposal`, `FactProposal`, `FactVoting`, `AiClaim`, `Challenge`, `FinalRecompute`, `Resolved`, `InvalidDeadend`. The dashboard's phase-filter chips group these into the browsing categories above. Presentation helpers (phase-to-label, relative deadline, digit grouping, hash previews) live in `app/src/lib/oracleView.ts`.
