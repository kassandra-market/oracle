---
title: "Crowdfunding a market"
description: "create_market, contribute, cancel, refund — the pre-create-below-floor → crowdfund-to-min_liquidity → funded model, the Contribution accounts, and the config-as-state snapshot."
---

Phase 1 of a market's life is pure crowdfunding: no MetaDAO accounts, no trading,
no pool. A creator stands the market up below the liquidity floor, anyone
contributes toward it, and — until activation — every contributor funds at the same
pre-pool basis. Deferring the heavy MetaDAO machinery to
[activation](/market/activation) keeps this phase cheap and keeps a never-funded
market trivially refundable.

## create_market (Ix 2)

`create_market` (`processor/create_market.rs`) is permissionless and lightweight.
It reads the `Config` singleton for the canonical KASS mint and current
`min_liquidity`/`fee_bps`, reads the Kassandra oracle as a gate, and then:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Validate the oracle binding">
    `outcome_index` must index a real option (`< oracle.options_count`) or the call
    fails `InvalidOutcome`; the oracle must still be non-terminal
    (`phase < Resolved`) or it fails `OracleResolved`
    (`create_market.rs:85-90`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Allocate the Market + escrow">
    The `Market` PDA `[b"market", oracle, [outcome_index]]` is created (one
    sub-market per outcome — a second attempt at the same address fails
    `InvalidAccount`), plus an SPL **escrow** token account
    `[b"escrow", market]` on KASS whose token authority is the Market PDA.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Seed the first contribution">
    The creator's `seed_amount` KASS transfers into escrow and their `Contribution`
    is recorded. `seed_amount` may be **any positive amount, even below the floor**;
    zero fails `ZeroAmount`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The written `Market` snapshots `min_liquidity` and `fee_bps` **from `Config` at
creation time** (`create_market.rs:166-167`), sets `total_contributed =
seed_amount`, `open_contributions = 1`, and `status = Funding`.

<Note title="Config-as-state">
Because `min_liquidity` and `fee_bps` are copied onto the `Market` at creation, an
in-flight market is immune to later governance changes — a futarchy
[`update_config`](/market-protocol/instructions) cannot move the goalposts on a
market people are already funding. This mirrors the Kassandra oracle's frozen
per-oracle parameter snapshots.
</Note>

## contribute (Ix 3)

Anyone stakes more KASS toward the floor with `contribute`
(`processor/contribute.rs`). The market must be `Funding` (else `NotFunding`) and
the escrow must match `market.escrow_vault`. The contributor's KASS transfers into
escrow and their `Contribution` is created or topped up.

The processor detects the create-vs-top-up branch **before** moving tokens: only a
brand-new `Contribution` increments `open_contributions`; a repeat top-up by an
existing contributor must not double-count (`contribute.rs:53-77`).
`total_contributed` grows by `amount` with checked math.

### Contribution accounts

Each `(market, contributor)` pair has exactly one `Contribution` PDA
`[b"contribution", market, contributor]` (`programs/kassandra-market/src/state.rs:120`)
recording `market`, `contributor`, `amount`, a `claimed` flag, and `bump`. It is
the record both `refund` and [`claim_lp`](/market/resolution) pay against, and
`open_contributions` on the `Market` counts the live ones — a rent-reclaim counter
that gates `close_market`.

## cancel (Ix 4) + refund (Ix 5)

If the oracle goes terminal before anyone activates the market, the crowdfund
unwinds:

- **`cancel`** (`processor/cancel.rs`) — permissionless; flips a `Funding` market
  to `Cancelled` once its oracle is terminal (`Resolved`/`InvalidDeadend`), at
  **any** funding level. This deliberately admits fully-funded markets whose oracle
  resolved before activation, which would otherwise strand their KASS.
- **`refund`** (`processor/refund.rs`) — permissionless per-contributor pull. It
  program-signs a transfer of `contribution.amount` out of escrow (authority = the
  Market PDA) back to the contributor's KASS account, then **closes** the
  `Contribution` (rent → contributor) and decrements `open_contributions`.

<Warning>
Because `refund` is permissionless, it verifies the destination token account's SPL
owner equals the recorded `contribution.contributor` (`refund.rs:68`) — otherwise a
cranker could redirect someone's refund. The closed `Contribution`'s absence is the
idempotency guard: a second refund can't even load it (`AlreadyClaimed` is now
unused).
</Warning>

Continue to [Activation](/market/activation) for the funded path, or see the
[reference instructions](/market-protocol/instructions) for exact account lists.
